FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about MyOldCherokee.com.

What is MyOldCherokee.com?

An independent directory and help hub for owners of older Jeep Cherokees. We focus on troubleshooting guides, DIY resources, and a directory of local shops that actually understand these vehicles.

Is this an official Jeep or Stellantis website?

No. MyOldCherokee.com is a completely independent resource. We are not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to Jeep, Stellantis, or any manufacturer.

How do I list my shop?

Submit your shop through the Submit a Shop page and we'll review it within 48 hours. We'll reach out if we need any additional information before adding your listing to the directory.

What Cherokee models are covered?

My Old Cherokee is centered on the Jeep Cherokee XJ, especially older models still on the road (or one good weekend away from being back on the road), if you know, you know. My Old Cherokee is a focused resource for troubleshooting, DIY repairs, repair shop listings and more.

How do I get my listing updated or removed?

Use the Contact page and send us your shop name, the change needed, and your contact email. We'll update or remove listings promptly.

Can I submit a guide or resource link?

Yes! We love community contributions. Use the Contact page to suggest a resource, guide link, or original how-to content.

Our Story

About MyOldCherokee.com

MyOldCherokee.com was built out of frustration. The kind that comes from spending three hours digging through forum threads trying to figure out why a 1997 Cherokee won't start, only to find conflicting advice, dead links, and zero nearby shops that know what they're doing.

The goal is simple: organize the most useful troubleshooting knowledge, DIY resources, and local Cherokee-savvy shops into one clean, searchable directory so the next owner in that same situation gets help in minutes, not hours.

We launched with U.S. coverage and a long-term plan to expand internationally as the community grows. If you're an owner, a mechanic, or just someone who refuses to give up on a great vehicle welcome.

Tips for Junkyard Hunting

Before You Go

Know Your VIN

Confirm Cherokee vs Cherokee Parts

Cherokee share some parts but not many. Know your exact year and model before you go certain engines, axles, and body clips are not interchangeable.

Bring Tools

Self-Service Yard Prep

Pull-a-part yards require your own tools. Bring basic sockets, wrenches, penetrating oil, and a flashlight. Call ahead to confirm they have Cherokee inventory before the trip.

Search Online First

Use Car-Part.com

Car-Part.com aggregates inventory from salvage yards nationwide. Search your part number before visiting to confirm availability and compare yard prices across your region.

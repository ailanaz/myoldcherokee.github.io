Buyer and Seller Due Diligence

Use this checklist as a starting point when buying or selling an older Cherokee. MyOldCherokee.com provides listings and information for reference only and does not verify third party claims.

Important

This checklist is general information only. It is not exhaustive and may not apply to every vehicle, part, seller, shop, yard, or situation. MyOldCherokee.com does not inspect, verify, certify, or guarantee listings, businesses, inventory, pricing, availability, or outcomes. If needed, consult qualified professionals before completing a transaction.

Buying a vehicle or parts

Focus on verification steps you can complete before paying.

✓
Verify VIN and title status
Confirm title is valid, matches the VIN, and is transferable.
✓
Confirm seller identity
Verify contact details and ownership authority.
✓
Inspect in person or hire an inspector
Do not rely on listing text alone.

Using a junk or salvage yard

Confirm details directly with the yard before traveling.

✓
Confirm inventory availability
Ask if a compatible Cherokee is currently on-site.
✓
Verify fitment before purchase
Confirm part compatibility for your exact year and configuration.
✓
Ask about policies
Pricing, returns, tools allowed, entry fees, warranty terms.

Junk and salvage yard listings are provided for informational purposes only. MyOldCherokee.com does not verify inventory, part availability, pricing, fitment, yard policies, business status, or outcomes. Users are responsible for confirming details directly and performing their own due diligence.

Using a repair shop

Choose shops based on your own verification, not directory inclusion.

✓
Verify qualifications independently
Confirm licensing, insurance, certifications, and experience.
✓
Get a written estimate
Confirm parts, labor, and fees before authorizing work.
✓
Confirm timeline and communication
Ask how updates, approvals, and changes are handled.
